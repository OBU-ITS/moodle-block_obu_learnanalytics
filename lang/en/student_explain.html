Oxford Brookes Learning Analytics

Information on your online engagement in Moodle (Virtual Learning Environment) and the Electronic Library is now available for you to monitor.

The objective is for students to be able to compare their engagement pattern against their own trend and their cohort. A cohort is the group of students at the same stage of a course.

Your engagement rating is calculated on a weekly basis using a combination of time spent online, consistency of access, activity while online and downloads. Weeks run from Monday to Sunday, so it defaults to showing you data for the last complete week.

We understand that not being online does not mean that you are not active, as depending on the course and your own preferences you can study from other sources. Your rating is not a score. We compare your weekly engagement to previous weeks so you can spot if your engagement has declined at points of the course where it shouldn't. By comparing your rating to the cohort average you can spot if others are finding material or activities that you may have missed.

The introduction panel that this link was on can be configured wherever you want it on your Moodle dashboard. It contains a brief summary with options to drill into the data and get suggestions.

The first summary point is comparing where you are relative to others in your cohort, by clicking on the available link you can see this as a bar chart divided into 'quartiles' with your own engagement highlighted. The Mean and Median averages are also shown for reference.

The second summary point is to indicate your own trend and clicking on the link shows you your pattern over the previous weeks, with options to change the units of measure.

The third summary point indicates your consistency of online engagement. Numerous studies show that a regular study pattern is better for knowledge transfer and retention, rather than a few very long sessions. This link is work in progress and shows your activity over the last 2 weeks showing (still being discussed and designed).

The lightbulb on the right of the panel shows suggestions for how you could improve your engagement. The contents will vary but does include an option to show what other resource sites that your cohort are regularly using.
