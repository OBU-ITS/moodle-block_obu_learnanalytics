Oxford Brookes Learning Analytics

The aim of the system is to use students’ digital footprint to generate actionable insights into how learners interact with the learning opportunities provided and thus help us to improve student learning.  We have used data that can be extracted from the VLE, Library information system, shibboleth and the student record system. The engagement ratings are calculated on a weekly basis using a combination of time spent online and activity.

We modelled a range of variables to see which could best predict a student’s average grade. We have used these variables in the dash boards.

Engagement patterns vary by programme and using the data you can see when students are engaged, the system allows you to see the engagement pattern for individual students.  As a reference point we have also included the average engagement pattern for the cohort.

Our analysis suggests that it cannot necessarily be said that an engaged student will have successful outcomes or that a less engaged student will not. It is important therefore that when looking at the dashboards not to make assumptions about how students are learning and instead use the  information to stimulate effective tutorial and academic discussions.

As a Student Support Coordinator you can see the Tutor's view for a Student. Enter a Student Number and Press Tutors View to look at the details for that students programme. (The Student View is currently disabled)

The top section has selection criteria to filter the results and change the date range, it will remember the last programme you looked at, but a different one can be selected from the drop down or the search button will allow you to enter a programme code. The weeks run from Monday to Sunday, so the dashboard defaults to showing the last complete week.

The dashboard shows a list of students with an indicator to their relative engagement within the selection and their own engagement trend over the last 3 weeks.  It defaults to showing the least engaged first, click on the position heading to reverse the sort.  Hover over the icons for further details.

The banding drop down will allow you to change the period for position comparison and the percentage used for grouping.

Click on a student to show a graph of their engagement compared to others.
